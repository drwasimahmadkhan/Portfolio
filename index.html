Professional Summary

Dr. Wasim Ahmad Khan is a PhD Computer Scientist specializing in Artificial Intelligence, intelligent agents, cloud robotics, NLP, and applied machine learning. His work bridges academic research and deployable AI systems, with experience in research publication, teaching, software architecture, AI solution design, and industry-oriented AI implementation.

His interests include LLM-based systems, retrieval-augmented generation, computer vision, cognitive agents, cloud AI, and responsible AI adoption. He is dedicated to pushing the boundaries of autonomous systems to create real-world, enterprise-grade impact.

Applied AI & Research Areas

Enterprise RAG Architectures

Designing custom Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) pipelines for organizations to securely interact with their internal knowledge bases. Utilizing vector databases, embedding models, and orchestration frameworks like LangChain to reduce hallucination and improve data discovery.

Cloud Robotics Frameworks

Research and implementation of task offloading and knowledge sharing in cloud-based robotic environments. Experience with frameworks like Rapuyta to enable low-latency, distributed computation for resource-constrained physical robots.

Vision Intelligence Systems

Developing computer vision applications for real-time object detection, anomaly recognition, and visual learning architectures. Focusing on deployable models optimized for edge devices and central cloud processing.

Cognitive Agent Simulation

Ph.D. Dissertation Focus

Creating cognitive architectures that allow software agents to generate motivational feelings, simulate top-down attention, and adapt to complex, unstructured data environments, paving the way for AGI research.
